Preparing for a presentation
When you prepare yourself forÂ Data analysis, presentation or reporting, it is important to understand your audience.
Following are few questions you must ask yourself for clarity and succession planning.
Who, What, and How?
Who: To whom are you planning to communicate with? it will help you to understand how your audience will perceive you.
What: Clarity on what do you want to convey to your audience.
How: How can you use data to help the audience understand your point?
Exploratory vs Explanatory Analysis
Let's understand the purpose of both Analysis types.
Exploratory Analysis
To understand the data you need to explore data. This helps you get familiar with data, Here you could understand about
Relationships and trends in your data
Origin of data
How is the data labeled?
Is data structured or unstructured?
Any gaps, trends or any relationships formed.
Thus exploring makes analyzing data easy. It is also important to know the questions you are trying to and this helps to understand which method you can use to analyze your data.
Explanatory Analysis
This is the outcome of your exploratory analysis.
These are the crucial points you have arrived after your exploration at and would want to share with your audience.
While sharing your findings, you need to be in explanatory phase.
Concentrate only on the information your audience needs to know.
Avoid explaining all the homework that you have done and just come to the important points of discussion.
