Clutter Free Reporting
How to declutter - step-by-step
Remove Chart Border:
Chart borders are always unnecessary, remove these chart borders and instead use the white space to differentiate the visual from other elements.
Remove gridlines:
If you think that your data will be processed more effectively and helps your audiences to trace their finger from the data to the axis, only in that case, try to keep light-colored or grey gridlines.
But it's always better to get rid of these gridlines as it gives good contrast. Focus just the data.
Remove Data Markers:
Every single element acts as a cognitive load on your audiences, Use Data markers only when you have a purpose.
It's always good to keep Data markers away and reduce cluttering.
Clean up Axis labels
Avoid trailing zeros (Eg: 1.00, 5.00) on Y-axis labels. They do not provide any information; instead, make the graph look complicated. It's always better to get rid of these.
You can use abbreviations likeÂ Jan, FebÂ on X-axis instead of complete names to eliminate diagonal text.
Label Data directly
Once you eliminate extraneous cognitive load, place the label directly next to the data that they describe it more presentable.
Leverage consistent color
Always try to maintain the consistent color for both data and labels. This gives a visual clue to audiences indicating thatÂ these two pieces of information are related.
